\question Given the following Bayes' net: \includegraphics[height=2in]{aima-fig-13_15_a-sprinkler-bayes-net.pdf}

What is the probability that the grass is wet, the sprinkler didn't run, it rained recently, and it is cloudy?

\begin{solution}[2.5in]
\begin{align*}
Pr(x_1, \dots, x_n) &= \prod_{i=1}^n Pr( x_i | parents(X_i)) \tag{13.2}\\
Pr(w, \neg s, r, c) &= Pr(w \mid \neg s, r) Pr(\neg s \mid c) Pr(r \mid c) Pr(c)\\
                    &= (0.90) (0.90) (0.80) (0.5) \\
                    &= (0.81) (0.45) \\
                    &= 0.324
\end{align*}

\end{solution}

\question Elsa is unlikely to chew up slippers, $P(ElsaChews) = 0.1$, Anna is more likely, $P(AnnaChews) = 0.3$.  If the slippers are chewed, whether she or her sister did it, Elsa is likely to go into her crate and look concerned, $P(ElsaConcerned \mid SlippersChewed) = 0.8$.  If you were constructing a Bayesian network, in which topological order would you include the variables $ElsaConcerned, SlippersChewed, ElsaChews, AnnaChews$?

\begin{center}
\includegraphics[height=1.25in]{elsa-concerned.jpg}
\end{center}

\begin{solution}[.25in]
\[
ElsaChews, AnnaChews, SlippersChewed, ElsaConcerned
\]

You could reverse the order of $ElsaChews$ and $AnnaChews$.
\end{solution}
